# Wino Business Model Canvas (BMC)

Last updated: 2026-04-01

## 0) Current stage note
Canonical status reference:
- `TRANSFORMATION_COMPLETE_SUMMARY.md`

This BMC should be read against the current truthful stage:
- technical scope is strong,
- product scope is meaningful,
- commercial proof is still emerging,
- and the product is better described as advanced pre-production / pilot-ready direction than full production release.

## 1) Customer Segments
### Primary user segment
- Algerian consumers looking for nearby, trustworthy products, promotions, and merchants.

### Primary merchant segment
- small and medium local merchants who need lightweight digital visibility, promotion, and customer acquisition tools.

### Secondary segments
- growth-oriented merchants willing to pay for visibility,
- local operators or admins managing moderation and merchant support,
- future ecosystem partners in payments, logistics, or distribution.

## 2) Value Propositions
### For users
- nearby discovery instead of generic listing overload,
- more confidence through reviews, reports, and trust cues,
- easier comparison across products, packs, and promotions,
- direct map directions to merchant locations when users decide to visit,
- practical local buying paths through direct contact, sharing, and QR.

### For merchants
- simple digital presence without marketplace complexity,
- local reach shaped by geography and relevance,
- built-in promotion and monetization pathways,
- clearer merchant-facing metrics than unstructured social selling.

### For the platform
- a unified product that combines discovery, trust, and growth instead of separating them across multiple tools.

## 3) Channels
- Android mobile app,
- deep links and short links,
- QR-based sharing,
- merchant referrals,
- social sharing from listings,
- field onboarding and direct merchant acquisition,
- university, startup, or local-commerce networks for early pilots.

## 4) Customer Relationships
- self-service browsing for users,
- guided onboarding for merchants,
- admin-assisted moderation and support during early stages,
- progressively more explainable analytics and lifecycle messaging,
- feedback-driven product iteration.

## 5) Revenue Streams
### Near-term
- merchant subscriptions,
- ad campaigns and sponsored visibility,
- coin-pack or wallet-based merchant spending.

### Mid-term
- premium merchant tools,
- promoted category placements,
- advanced reporting or campaign assistance services.

### Important note
For the current stage, revenue should be introduced gradually. The strongest early goal is merchant activation and retention, not maximizing extraction too early.

## 6) Key Resources
- the Wino codebase and product IP,
- merchant and catalog content,
- trust and moderation data,
- analytics and recommendation infrastructure,
- multilingual UX groundwork,
- admin and support processes,
- brand positioning around local trust-aware commerce.

## 7) Key Activities
- merchant onboarding and activation,
- product publishing and catalog quality control,
- trust and abuse moderation,
- improving ranking, nearby discovery, and relevance,
- maintaining notifications and deep links,
- supporting monetization flows,
- localization and UX improvement,
- measuring retention, trust, and merchant outcomes.

## 8) Key Partners
- payment ecosystem players and financial rails,
- cloud, messaging, and notification providers,
- merchant communities and local business networks,
- universities, incubators, or startup support structures,
- future logistics or delivery partners if the product expands operational scope.

## 9) Cost Structure
- mobile and backend engineering,
- hosting, database, and messaging infrastructure,
- moderation and support operations,
- merchant acquisition and onboarding,
- quality assurance and release management,
- future compliance, payment, and business-development overhead.

## 10) Why This Model Fits Algeria
This BMC fits the Algerian context because it does not assume:
- immediate full online checkout maturity,
- highly technical merchants,
- high trust by default,
- platform-independent user behavior.

Instead, it fits a local O2O market where discovery happens digitally, but decision-making still depends heavily on proximity, trust, and direct communication.

## 11) Strategic Advantage
Wino's strongest structural advantages are:
- `Store == User`, which reduces merchant onboarding friction,
- nearby and area-aware discovery rather than flat inventory exposure,
- trust/report/review integration as part of the core product,
- built-in merchant growth pathways through ads, subscriptions, and wallet mechanisms,
- multilingual support better aligned with Algerian usage realities.

## 12) Web-Backed Context
The BMC is supported by external market conditions:
- DataReportal reports 37.8 million internet users in Algeria and 27.5 million social media identities based on late-2025 data.
- SATIM reports growing digital payment infrastructure with 51,000+ terminals and 500+ web merchants.
- APS reported official pressure to facilitate e-commerce development while preserving digital security on 2025-12-14.

These sources support the market opportunity, while Wino's architecture addresses the remaining trust and usability gap.
